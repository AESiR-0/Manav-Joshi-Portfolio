The problem with your task list

Your to-do app knows your habits, your meals, your worries at 2am. That does not belong in a startup's database.

Software you own. Data you own. No server of ours. No account with us. No pricing page. You deploy Kaka once and it answers to you.

When you stop renting your tools, nothing expires. The app stays up because your Cloudflare account stays up. Your data sits in your storage. If we disappear tomorrow, your Kaka doesn't notice.

Why "Kaka"

Kaka is Gujarati for uncle. Every Indian household has one. He remembers your deadlines before you do, asks whether you went to the gym, and tells you the truth on Sunday whether you asked for it or not.

That is the app. It captures whatever you throw at it, nags you at exactly the right moment, and writes you an honest review of your week. Warm, but never soft on you.

What it does

01Speak, don't fill forms

"Call the accountant Friday 4pm, remind me an hour before" becomes exactly that. Brain dumps get split into items. Goals get broken into first moves instead of rotting as one vague todo.

02Three things each morning

Kaka pre-picks the three tasks that matter most today, using due dates, importance, and age. One tap accepts. The day starts decided.

03Pillars, not categories

Define the areas every day should touch. The AI files each task silently, and a chip lights up when you feed a pillar. You see a starved pillar before it becomes a starved month.

04Reminders that reach you

Real push notifications on your phone, on by default for every dated task. Add Kaka to your home screen and it behaves like any app.

05A weekly review with opinions

Every Monday, the AI reads your week and writes you 4 to 6 honest sentences. What moved, what slipped, what keeps getting pushed. Then one specific suggestion.

06It files your life

Meals, workouts, sleep, learnings, streaks with XP on top. Say it in one line and Kaka puts it where it belongs.

The second brain loop

Your notes app keeps the memory. Kaka runs the day. It feeds your second brain, automatically.

GET /api/digest One day of your activity as clean markdown, plus every learning you captured. Pipe it into Obsidian, Notion, or a plain journal folder.
POST /api/context Push your goals and projects up, so Kaka's AI knows who it is talking to when it files and advises.
GET /api/review The latest weekly review as JSON, ready to archive.

Every other to-do app is a silo. It knows what you did and tells nobody. Kaka closes the loop: your vault learns what you actually did today, without you writing it up.

Setup

The 2026 way

  1. Download the folder above.
  2. Open it in Claude Code, Cursor, or any coding agent.
  3. Say "set up my Kaka".

The repo carries a runbook your agent follows. It does everything itself and stops only where a human is required: you click one Allow button and type two secrets into your own terminal. It never sees your password or your key. About ten minutes.

The manual way

# free Cloudflare account + free Gemini key, then:
npm install
npx wrangler login
npx wrangler kv namespace create KAKA_KV
npm run build && npx wrangler deploy
npx wrangler secret put SITE_PASSWORD
npx wrangler secret put GEMINI_API_KEY

Runs comfortably inside Cloudflare's and Google's free tiers. Monthly cost: nothing.

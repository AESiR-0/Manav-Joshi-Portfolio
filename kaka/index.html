Ownership

Software you own. Data you own.

Your to-do app knows your habits, your meals, your worries at 2am. That does not belong in a startup's database.

No server of ours

Kaka runs as a worker on your own Cloudflare account. If we disappear tomorrow, your Kaka doesn't notice.

No account with us

One password, chosen by you, stored by you. Your data sits in your storage and nowhere else.

No pricing page

It fits inside Cloudflare's and Google's free tiers. When you stop renting your tools, nothing expires.

What it does

Smart to-dos and habits.

✎

Speak, don't fill forms

"Call the accountant Friday 4pm, remind me an hour before" becomes exactly that. Brain dumps get split into items. Goals get broken into first moves.

☰

Three things each morning

Kaka pre-picks the three tasks that matter most today, using due dates, importance, and age. One tap accepts. The day starts decided.

◈

Pillars, not categories

Define the areas every day should touch. The AI files each task silently, and a chip lights up when you feed a pillar.

◷

Reminders that reach you

Real push notifications on your phone, on by default for every dated task. Add Kaka to your home screen and it behaves like any app.

✦

A weekly review with opinions

Every Monday, the AI reads your week and writes you 4 to 6 honest sentences. What moved, what slipped, then one specific suggestion.

✓

It files your life

Meals, workouts, sleep, learnings, streaks with XP on top. Say it in one line and Kaka puts it where it belongs.

The second brain loop

Your notes app keeps the memory.
Kaka runs the day.

Every other to-do app is a silo. It knows what you did and tells nobody. Kaka closes the loop: your vault learns what you actually did today, without you writing it up.

GET /api/digest

One day of your activity as clean markdown, plus every learning you captured. Pipe it into Obsidian, Notion, or a plain journal folder.

POST /api/context

Push your goals and projects up, so Kaka's AI knows who it is talking to when it files and advises.

GET /api/review

The latest weekly review as JSON, ready to archive.

# this morning, automatically
$ curl kaka.you.dev/api/digest?date=2026-07-19
# 2026-07-19 — Kaka daily
## Done
- Ship the pricing page draft
- Morning run (movement)
## Learnings captured
- Sam is a potential client
→ saved to vault/log/2026-07-19.md

Why "Kaka"

Kaka is Gujarati for uncle. Every Indian household has one. He remembers your deadlines before you do, asks whether you went to the gym, and tells you the truth on Sunday whether you asked for it or not.

That is the app. It captures whatever you throw at it, nags you at exactly the right moment, and writes you an honest review of your week. Warm, but never soft on you.

Setup

Ten minutes, once.

The 2026 way

Let your AI do it

  1. Download the folder above.
  2. Open it in Claude Code, Cursor, or any coding agent.
  3. Say "set up my Kaka".

The repo carries a runbook your agent follows. It stops only where a human is required: you click one Allow button and type two secrets into your own terminal. It never sees your password or your key.

The manual way

Six commands

# free Cloudflare account + free Gemini key, then:
npm install
npx wrangler login
npx wrangler kv namespace create KAKA_KV
npm run build && npx wrangler deploy
npx wrangler secret put SITE_PASSWORD
npx wrangler secret put GEMINI_API_KEY

Runs comfortably inside Cloudflare's and Google's free tiers. Monthly cost: nothing.

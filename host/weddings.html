Why me

A month before the wedding, I sit down with the couple. The script, the games, the bits come from your story. No template.

Then the wedding does what weddings do. The bride breaks the glass tower. An uncle starts a lecture on stage. Someone slips. I’ve had all three. The room still remembers a great night, because a host who can improvise doesn’t panic. He plays it.

I don’t ask people to scream. I give them a reason to.
